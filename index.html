A Head "Turner" in the World of Genetics

Author: Oluwademilade Thamrat Uthman

Date Published: 13/09/24

Turner syndrome is a chromosomal disorder that affects females, occurring when one of the X chromosomes is missing or partially missing. This condition affects about 1 in 2,500 female births worldwide.

Key features of Turner syndrome include short stature, delayed puberty, infertility, and distinct physical characteristics such as a webbed neck and low-set ears. Many individuals also experience heart and kidney problems, as well as learning difficulties, particularly in spatial reasoning and math.

Turner syndrome is typically diagnosed through a karyotype test, which analyzes chromosomes. Although there is no cure, early intervention with growth hormone therapy and estrogen replacement can help manage symptoms. Regular monitoring and treatment for associated medical issues are essential.

With proper medical care and support, most individuals with Turner syndrome can lead healthy, productive lives. Genetic counseling can provide valuable information and support for affected families.
